(Catalogue ref SP 16/506/45)

[Extra explanations are in square brackets.]

It is ordered by the Lords and Commons assembled in Parliament, for the defence of the king and Parliament and the true Protestant religion and the laws and freedoms of the kingdom, that an army immediately be raised, made up of:

  • 6600 men on horse, to be put into 11 regiments;
  • And 1000 men who can fight on foot or on horse, to be put into 10 companies;
  • And 14,400 foot soldiers, to be put into 12 regiments, each foot regiment to be made up of 1200, and to be put into 10 companies.

This army shall be paid according to the arrangement to be made by both Houses of Parliament, and shall be under the immediate command of Sir Thomas Fairfax, Knight, …

And it is further ordered by the Lords and Commons, that from the 1st of February 1644, the sums of money mentioned below shall be taxed and paid for the payment of the army and related costs in the following way.

That is: There shall be a monthly charge, until the 1st of December next, if the war goes on so long, upon:

  • The city of London and the county of Middlesex - £8059 and 15 shillings
  • The county of Essex - £6750 per month
  • The county of Suffolk - £7070 per month
  • The county of Norfolk - £7070 per month
  • The city and county of Norwich - £366 per month
  • The county of Lincoln - £2070 per month
  • The county of Hereford - £2432 and 10 shillings per month
  • The Isle of Ely - £728, 13 shillings and 4 pence per month
  • The county of Cambridge - £2171, six shillings and 8 pence per month
  • The county of Huntingdon - £1020 per month
  • The county of Kent, with the city and county of Canterbury and the Cinque-ports - £7070 per month
  • The county of Surrey, with the borough of Southwark - £2000 per month
  • The county of Sussex - £3927, fifteen shillings, six pence, three farthings per month
  • The county of Derby - £516 per month
  • The county of Rutland - £184 per month
  • The county of Warwick - £300 per month
  • The county of Leicester - £250 per month
  • The county of Northampton - £450 per month
  • The county of Bedford - £1000 per month

And so that these sums of money may be taxed and paid, it is further ordered by the Lords and Commons that the Committees appointed for the cities, counties and places mentioned shall meet together (at some convenient place in each of the cities, counties and places) within eight days of this order being published. Afterwards, at least seven of them shall meet (at such places as they think fit) at least twice every week, to quickly put this order into action; …

And at once the Committees are authorised and required to appoint whatever number of people they think fit within their areas to be assessors of the rates. These people are authorised and required to assess every person within their area who has any land or money, [to pay] according to the rates mentioned in this order.

[In old money: 4 farthings equalled one penny, 12 pence equalled one shilling and 20 shillings equalled £1.]
